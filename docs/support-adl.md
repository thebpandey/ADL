# Support ADL

ADL is **free and open-source**, and it always will be. Every guide,
reference, and troubleshooting page is available to everyone at no cost —
**donations are entirely optional** and never required to use the
documentation or to get help.

If ADL saved you time or taught you something, and you'd like to support the
work, here are the ways to do it.

## Where support goes

Donations help cover the time spent testing on real hardware, keeping guides
current as Termux, Ubuntu, and Android evolve, and maintaining the site.

## Donor recognition

Sponsors and donors are gratefully acknowledged:

- **GitHub Sponsors** appear in the sponsors section of the
  [README](https://github.com/thebpandey/ADL#support-adl) and in the
  recognition banner on the documentation homepage (updated automatically).
- If you donate through Buy Me a Coffee or Cash App and would like to be
  recognized, open an issue or mention it in
  [Discussions](https://github.com/thebpandey/ADL/discussions) and you'll be
  added.
- Prefer to stay anonymous? Just say so — recognition is opt-in.

## Other ways to help

Money isn't the only way to support ADL — these matter just as much:

- **Star the repository** on [GitHub](https://github.com/thebpandey/ADL)
- **Report bugs** and unclear instructions
- **Contribute** fixes and guides — see the
  [contributing guide](https://github.com/thebpandey/ADL/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md)
- **Share your device reports** so more phones get documented
- **Tell someone** who'd enjoy turning their phone into a Linux desktop
